Racing Lines & Exits

There are lots of things to do in Dubai, so on my weekend I decided to do a race week. First stop, Dubai Autodrome. Everything there is designed amazingly. Small karts, hot tyres, everyone looking to outdo a lap — it was a great replica of an F1 track.

I was mainly looking at following my racing lines, and my exits. Every corner mattered. One tiny mistake and you lose time. And who would wanna lose to their friend now would they? — saying who had the fastest lap and who blew the last corner. Most of my laps were done well but I swerved on a pretty hard corner.

No Wrestling on Lifts

Sunday we changed the whole thing but still racing. I went to the Mall of the Emirates with some friends and went directly into Ski Dubai. I loved the cold. It reminded me of the snow covered mountains in the Himalayas.

As someone who had done ski racing it was no bother than going on a pretty small track. I went on the comfy ski lift with my friend where he started to wrestle me when I was like 50ft in the air — pretty dumb. But either way once we reached the top we went down like a cheetah. The skiing was really fun at the cost of getting a cold.
